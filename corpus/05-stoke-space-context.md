# Stoke Space context (for interview grounding)

**Stoke Space** (often referred to publicly as Stoke Space Technologies) is an aerospace company working on reusable launch and related technologies. Public reporting discusses vertically integrated design, manufacturing, and test, and a focus on **high-cadence iteration** for hardware.

Boltline is frequently described as **originally internal tooling** created to manage the complexity of real rocket development—where shop-floor realities, supply constraints, and test feedback collide with design intent. That origin story supports a credible product thesis: tools that survive **actual builds** tend to encode practical workflows rather than theoretical ones.

In interviews, a concise way to connect Stoke and Boltline is:

- Stoke’s hardware programs generate **dense operational data** and **strict traceability needs**.
- Boltline generalizes lessons from that environment into a product for **other** hardware teams facing similar complexity.
