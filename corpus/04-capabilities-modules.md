# Capabilities and modules (high level)

Public descriptions of Boltline and related announcements commonly highlight several functional areas. Wording varies by page and date, but the themes below are useful for a demo corpus organized into retrievable chunks.

## Parts and BOM-style structure

Managing **part masters**, relationships between parts, and **engineering BOMs (eBOMs)** so that assemblies are navigable and changes propagate coherently.

## Inventory and locations

Tracking **where** material is and **how** it moves through manufacturing, sometimes described alongside labeling or scanning workflows (for example QR-driven tracking in public materials).

## Work plans / procedures

**Operator-facing workflows**: step-by-step build and test procedures, with structured instructions rather than only static documents.

## Orders and supply chain touchpoints

**Order management** connected to inventory and planning, often with integrations to broader business systems (ERP) in enterprise deployments.

## Automation / workflows

**No-code or low-code automation** to reduce manual handoffs between engineering, manufacturing, and supply chain functions—especially for recurring processes.

These modules matter for RAG because user questions are often object-specific (“How do we handle a BOM line change?”) and benefit from retrieving the right policy chunk.
