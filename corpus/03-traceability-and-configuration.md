# Traceability and configuration

**Traceability** in Boltline’s public materials means linking requirements, parts, builds, tests, and outcomes so a team can answer: what was built, from what, by whom, under which revision, and with what evidence.

**As-built configuration** is emphasized as a first-class concept: the system of record should reflect reality on the shop floor and in the field, not only the idealized design baseline.

Practical implications often discussed in enterprise hardware tooling apply here:

- **Revision control** for parts and assemblies, so “which revision is on this unit?” is answerable.
- **Lot and location awareness** where inventory and serialization matter.
- **Evidence capture** from test and inspection, supporting investigations and compliance narratives.

When discussing RAG in an interview, you can connect this section to why retrieval matters: policies, work instructions, and BOM lines are high-stakes text—grounding answers in an internal corpus reduces hallucinations and supports auditability of AI-assisted workflows.
