# Design, build, test, trace, improve

Boltline’s public narrative organizes hardware work into a **continuous loop** rather than a one-way handoff:

## Design

Teams may start from CAD, sketches, or parts already on hand. The platform is described as carrying **part relationships** forward so that downstream steps are not disconnected from upstream decisions.

## Build

Manufacturing and assembly are supported with **guided procedures** (often described in terms of work instructions, checklists, and visual aids). **Inventory** is expected to stay aligned with what is consumed and produced on the floor.

## Test

Testing validates what works and what does not. The product messaging allows **BOM changes** even late in the process—including in the field—reflecting that hardware programs rarely freeze specifications perfectly on the first try.

## Trace

Traceability is a headline theme: formalizing **parts, assemblies, and final products** with **as-built configuration** data. This supports audit readiness, failure analysis, and customer support.

## Improve

Improvement is framed as ongoing: refining products **before, during, or after delivery**, with an emphasis on knowing how to **service** what you ship.

This loop is useful RAG content because interview questions often map to one stage (e.g., “How do you handle field changes?”) while the product story is explicitly cross-stage.
